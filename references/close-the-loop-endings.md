# Close the loop endings

Use this when a draft ends with a generic conclusion or weak CTA.

## Principle

The final line should make the opening feel inevitable.

The ending does not need to summarize. It needs to land.

## Workflow

1. Identify the hook’s core image, phrase, or emotional claim.
2. Identify what changed in the middle: proof, insight, transformation, or decision.
3. Bring the hook back in a changed state.
4. Tie it to the action, lesson, or role fit.
5. Cut any generic closing sentence after it.

## Good endings

A good ending:

- echoes the opening
- sharpens the central idea
- gives the reader a next step or final thought
- feels earned by the proof
- stops before over-explaining

## Bad endings

Avoid:

- I would be happy to discuss
- Feel free to reach out
- In conclusion
- The possibilities are endless
- Thank you for your time and consideration
- Let me know if you have questions

## Patterns

### Hook image return

```text
Opening: The best CTA I saw this week was not a button.
Ending: Sometimes the best CTA is the sentence that makes the button feel obvious.
```

### Application ending

```text
Opening: A good ad does not always start with an idea. Sometimes it starts with a detail.
Ending: That is why I want this role: to turn the right detail into work people remember, and results clients can measure.
```

### Outreach ending

```text
If useful, I can send the 3 fixes I noticed.
```

Short. Specific. Easy to answer.
