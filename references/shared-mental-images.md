# Shared mental images

Use this when a piece needs a memorable opening, concept rationale, speech, application letter, ad, or brand idea.

## Principle

A familiar object can carry meaning faster than explanation.

A shoe can stand for determination.
An apple can stand for creativity.
A kitchen table can stand for family decisions.
A silent inbox can stand for missed opportunity.

The reader completes the meaning. That makes them participate.

## When to use

- job applications
- speeches
- pitch openings
- LinkedIn posts
- ad concepts
- brand manifestos
- founder stories
- campaign rationales

## Workflow

1. Identify the emotional world of the piece.
2. List objects, rituals, places, or moments the audience already understands.
3. Pick one image that carries the central idea.
4. Open with the image before explaining it.
5. Use the middle as proof.
6. Return to the image in the ending.

## Good image criteria

A strong shared image is:

- concrete
- culturally recognizable for the audience
- connected to the offer or role
- simple enough to explain in one sentence
- rich enough to echo at the end

## Avoid

- forced metaphors
- puns in every line
- explaining the reference too much
- choosing a favorite image instead of the right image
- using brand references the audience may not know

## Example pattern

```text
A receipt is not just proof you bought something.

Sometimes it is proof you trusted the page enough to stop comparing.

That is the job of conversion copy.
```
