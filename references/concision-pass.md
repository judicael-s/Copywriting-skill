# Concision pass

Use this after drafting or when the user asks for sharper copy.

## Principle

Shorter is better only when meaning and persuasion survive.

Cut until the sentence becomes weaker. Then restore the last useful word.

## Cut first

- filler: really, very, quite, basically, actually, just, maybe, kind of
- throat-clearing: I think that, it is important to note, one of the things, in order to
- duplicated meaning: each and every, future plans, end result
- weak hedges unless credibility needs them
- passive constructions when active is clearer
- abstract nouns that can become verbs
- generic modifiers: powerful, innovative, seamless, robust

## Rewrite patterns

| Weak | Strong |
|---|---|
| I think that organic growth is really important because it can help you get more qualified visitors. | Organic growth matters because it brings qualified visitors. |
| In order to improve conversion, you need to make the offer more clear. | To improve conversion, make the offer clearer. |
| Our solution helps businesses streamline their workflow. | Teams close tickets faster with fewer handoffs. |

## Pass order

1. Cut warm-up sentences.
2. Replace vague nouns with concrete nouns.
3. Replace abstract nouns with verbs.
4. Move proof closer to the claim.
5. Split sentences that carry two ideas.
6. Remove repeated benefits.
7. Read aloud.
8. Restore any word that carried meaning, tone, or credibility.

## Output rule

When asked for a concision edit, return:

- the revised copy
- optionally, 3 to 5 specific cuts and why they improved the line

Do not explain the full strategy unless asked.
